Thank you for your participation in the first part of this experiment. We move on to an individual component that is described to you below.

In the questions that follow, you are going to be asked to make ten decisions. Each decision will be between Option A and Option B. One of the ten choices you make will be randomly selected to determine your earnings for this part of the experiment.

After making all ten choices, the computer will randomly select one of the and display which decision you chose. The computer will "roll" a 10-sided die to produce a number between one and ten. The die-roll will determine the outcome of the selected lottery.

The following are examples of how your earnings are determined.

Example 1: Suppose the computer randomly selected the following decision.

Options
A B Your Choice
$1 or $3 each with probability 1/2 $0.1 with probability 9/10 or $5 with probability 1/10 A or B

Suppose that for this decision you selected Option A. If the computer "rolls" any number 1 through 5, you will receive $3. If the computer "rolls" any number 6 through 10, you will receive $1.

Example 2: Suppose the computer randomly selected the following decision.

Options
A B Your Choice
$1 or $3 each with probability 1/2 $0.1 with probability 3/10 or $5 with probability 7/10 A or B

Suppose that for this decision you selected B. If the computer "rolls" any number 1 through 7, you will receive $5. If the computer rolls 8 through 10, you will receive $0.1.

You may choose Option A for some decisions and Option B for others. Your earnings for this part of the experiment will be added to your earnings from the other part of the experiment and included in your cash payment.

If you have any questions, please raise your hand. If not feel free to proceed to the next page.
